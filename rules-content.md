# Preface

The Ember League is a community-run League of Legends competition that aims to deliver a structured, fair, and exciting competitive experience. This rulebook applies to all participating teams, players, coaches, and representatives, and governs all operations for the current Ember League season. The Ember League is not affiliated with or endorsed by Riot Games.

All official communication, coordination, and dispute resolution will be handled via the [Ember League Discord server](https://discord.gg/3PJrm96P9J).

Rule amendments may be made during the season, but will only apply if they do not retroactively impact already-played matches. Any such changes will be underlined and announced in a public changelog. The League Admins reserve the right to act in the best interests of competitive integrity and may issue penalties or decisions outside the bounds of this document if necessary. Lack of verbiage in this document does not constrain the League Admins and they may use any punitive actions at their disposal to act in the best interests of the League.

# Section 1 - Entry & Fee

## 1.1 Divisions

**Magma Division** — Diamond League
- Rank Cap: Masters 100 LP (peak)
- Entry Fee: $100 per team

**Scorch Division** — Emerald League
- Rank Cap: Diamond 4 99 LP (peak)
- Entry Fee: $80 per team

**Blaze Division** — Platinum League
- Rank Cap: Emerald 4 99 LP (peak)
- Entry Fee: $60 per team

**Cinder Division** — IBSG League
- Rank Cap: Platinum 4 99 LP (peak)
- Entry Fee: $50 per team

Each division operates independently but follows the same structure, rules, and procedures unless otherwise specified. If a new team joins the league while the split is in progress, the buy-in amount required is half of the original entry fee. *Only applicable if a team was to drop or forfeit the split.*

### 1.1.1 Refundable Deposit

All teams are required to submit an additional $30 refundable deposit at the beginning of the split. This deposit is returned at the end of the split under the following conditions:

- The team does not withdraw from the league after being accepted.
- The team plays out all scheduled matches through the regular season.

If a team withdraws or is removed for negative reasons (e.g. rule violations, conduct issues) or league-approved extenuating circumstances, the deposit is forfeited.

Organizations that have participated in Ember League play for two (2) splits or more in a row are exempt from this refund deposit.

For more information on how this deposit is lost or maintained, refer to Section 1.3.

## 1.2 Forfeits

### 1.2.1 Definition of a Forfeit

A forfeit occurs when a team is unable or unwilling to play a scheduled match, resulting in an automatic loss for the forfeiting team.

### 1.2.2 Conditions for a Forfeit

A team may be considered to have forfeited under the following conditions:

- **No Show:** Failure to have at least the minimum number of eligible players in the lobby within 15 minutes of the scheduled match time. (See Section 6.4)
- **Inability to Field a Team:** Inability to meet the roster requirements due to insufficient players or lack of substitutes.
- **Refusal to Play:** Explicitly refusing to play in written or verbal communication regardless of the reasoning.
- **Technical Inability:** Persistent technical issues that prevent a team from starting or continuing a match after all reasonable troubleshooting efforts have been exhausted.
- **Disqualification or Withdrawal:** Any team disqualified, withdrawn from, or otherwise removed from the tournament will automatically forfeit any and all future scheduled games in the tournament.

### 1.2.3 Forfeit Policy

To maintain the competitive integrity of the Ember League, the following policies apply:

- **First Forfeit Warning:** A team's first forfeit will result in a formal warning and a review of their status within the league.
- **Multiple Forfeits:** Two forfeits within a regular season may result in the team being removed from the league and disqualified from winnings, pending review by Ember League officials.
- **Playoff Forfeits:** Any team that forfeits during the playoffs will be immediately removed from the league and disqualified from winnings, and their opponent will advance to the next round.

### 1.2.4 Forfeit Communication

Teams anticipating a forfeit must notify Ember League Officials as well as their opponents as soon as they think forfeiting could be a possibility.

### 1.2.5 Exceptional Circumstances

In cases of emergencies or unavoidable circumstances, Ember League Officials may waive or modify forfeit penalties at their discretion. Appeals must be submitted within 24 hours of the forfeited match.

## 1.3 Loss of Deposit

The deposit provided upon sign up is "in good faith" and will be returned as long as teams follow these requirements:

- Finish out the season. If the team were to forfeit in its last week the deposit is lost.
- No major rules were violated.
- No punishments for cheating or smurfing.
- Teams that withdraw before Week 2 will be refunded their deposit.
- Withdrawals after Week 2 forfeit the deposit.
- If the team forfeits 2 individual games or 1 full Best of 3, a $5 deduction is placed on the deposit.
- If the team forfeits more than 4 individual games or 2 full Best of 3's the entire deposit is lost.

In the event of a team withdrawal, Admins will attempt to replace withdrawn teams with waitlisted teams if possible.

# Section 2 - Eligibility

## 2.1 Rank Restrictions

Players are only eligible if their highest peak rank falls within the Original Cap of their division based on the most recent three ranked seasons: The current season (Season 16), Season 15 (S2025), and Season 14 Split 3 (S2024 S3).

### 2.1.1 Original Cap

| Division | Cap |
|----------|-----|
| Magma | Masters 100 LP or lower |
| Scorch | Diamond 4 99 LP or lower |
| Blaze | Emerald 4 99 LP or lower |
| Cinder | Platinum 4 99 LP or lower |

Players may climb beyond the Original Cap after being added to the roster, but if they exceed the Absolute Growth Cap, they will be removed from the roster without exception.

### 2.1.2 Absolute Cap

| Division | Removal Threshold |
|----------|-------------------|
| Magma | Above Masters 300 LP |
| Scorch | Master 0 LP |
| Blaze | Diamond 4 0 LP |
| Cinder | Emerald 4 0 LP |

Absolute cap will not apply once playoffs begin. Players will be allowed to freely climb once regular season is over and the rank they reach will apply to next split.

### 2.1.3 Historic Eligibility

Players with a peak rank higher than the current division cap in past seasons may be deemed eligible to play, pending approval by an admin.

**Magma League**
- Masters 101 LP Peak (S2025)
- Masters 200 LP Peak (S2024 S3)
- Masters 250 LP Peak (Any split before S2024 S3)
- Masters 300 LP is the Absolute Growth Cap for this division.

**Scorch League**
- D3 0 LP Peak (S2025)
- D2 0 LP Peak (S2024 S3)
- Masters 0 LP Peak (Any split before S2024 S3)
- D1 99 LP is the Absolute Growth Cap for this division.

**Blaze League**
- E3 0 LP Peak (S2025)
- E2 0 LP Peak (S2024 S3)
- D3 0 LP Peak (Any split before S2024 S3)
- E1 99 LP Peak is the Absolute Growth Cap for this division.

**Cinder League**
- P3 0 LP Peak (S2025)
- P2 0 LP Peak (S2024 S3)
- E3 0 LP Peak (Any split before S2024 S3)
- P1 99 LP Peak is the Absolute Growth Cap for this division.

> **Note:** If the majority (3 of 5 players) of any given team in any given Ember League were to exceed the Original Cap of their league by more than 2 divisions, they will be subject to an in-depth review. If the vast majority (4 or more players) exceed the Original Cap of their league by more than 2 divisions, they will be automatically disqualified. This is to avoid rank sitting pre-split as much as possible.

### 2.1.4 "Volcanic" Player Status

A player can be given Volcanic status in 1 of 2 ways: competitive performance in Ember and other Amateur Leagues, or exceeding Historic Eligibility. A team may not have more than **ONE (1)** Volcanic Player in any given game (playing), and **TWO (2)** total on their roster. The list of Volcanic players is a living list and players can be added or removed at any given time, but typically players will be added in the off-season.

**Historic Eligibility**

Players who exceed their Ember League's Historic Eligibility (2.1.3) in previous splits may be permitted to join with a team as a Volcanic Player. A player may exceed Historic Eligibility by ONE (1) division for any or all of the given splits and be accepted as a Volcanic Player. For any split before the Historic Eligibility, they may have gone up to their Ember League's Absolute Cap and still be accepted as Volcanic.

**Competitive Performance**

At the end of each split, the League Staff will get together and determine any players who were outliers as Volcanic based purely off of in-game performance in Ember and any other Amateur Competitive League. This is an opinion based decision that will be made by the League Admin of each League and verified by the Commissioner to avoid any favoritism or targeting.

## 2.2 Minimum Account Requirements

- Level 200 or higher for Scorch & Magma
- Level 160 or higher for Blaze
- Level 140 or higher for Cinder
- At least 2 years of ranked activity
- At least a minimum of 100 games in the previous year
- Must meet solo queue scaling requirements
- Must disclose all ranked accounts (including region-transferred or alternate accounts) via Eligibility Bot
- Each player must declare a primary account that will be used to evaluate eligibility, match play, and solo queue mandates (see Section 5.1)
- The primary account must be on the NA server
- The primary account must be disclosed during roster registration. Requests to change the primary account must be submitted via Discord ticket.
- Any account provided, primary or alternate, may be used in game day matches. If your match is being streamed, you **MUST** use your primary account.
- Discord account must be 1 year old or older

## 2.3 Account Disclosure

All players must disclose their main account, and any alternate accounts, used for ranked play in the past 5 years. Account sharing is strictly prohibited. Players must provide accurate summoner names and report any name changes. If a player's undisclosed account is discovered, their team will lose all 5 bans in their next series, regardless of what series it is (this includes any playoff series).

## 2.4 Account Linking & Verification

All players must link their League of Legends account to Discord using the LeagueVettingBot with the `/link-account` command prior to being considered eligible for roster approval. Unlinked accounts will not be reviewed.

- Ember League uses **OP.GG** as the primary source for verifying rank history, game count, and solo queue activity.
- **League of Graphs** is used to confirm a player's peak rank for historical eligibility checks. Inaccurate or hidden data may lead to disqualification.

### 2.4.1 Skirting Clause

Any player found to be intentionally skirting around any rule with the intent of gaining an unfair advantage will be subject to penalties as the League sees fit. The lack of language in any other rule does not prevent a player found to be abusing rules from being penalized. This includes intentionally losing games to stay within a certain elo bracket, hiding accounts, or anything else that is deemed unfair even if it technically does not break any other rules.

# Section 3 - Team Requirements

## 3.1 Team Rosters

- Teams must have at least 5 players and no more than 10 players.
- Each team must have at least 1 designated Team Captain and 1 Team Owner.
- Team Owners are responsible for the team's entry fee and management.
- Team Captains are responsible for any day to day team activities like roster changes, scheduling issues, and anything the League needs from the Team.

## 3.2 Team Ownership

**Ownership Transfers:** Team ownership may be transferred mid-season by notifying the League Administration via ticket.

**Owner or Captain Departure:**
- If a team owner leaves mid-season, the team may continue competing only if a new person of authority is appointed by the Team Captain.
- If the team has already paid, participation may continue without the original owner, provided proper communication and approval occur.
- If a team captain leaves mid-season, the Team Owner will decide on a new team captain. The team owner has complete and final say on all team matters.

### 3.3.2 Logo Restrictions

- No copyrighted or trademarked material from other brands, esports organizations, or companies.
- No real-life individuals or celebrity likenesses may be used.
- No offensive, discriminatory, or inappropriate imagery is allowed.
- Logos must not resemble official Riot Games branding to avoid confusion.

### 3.3.3 Branding & Naming Policies

- Team names must be unique and cannot impersonate existing professional organizations.
- No profanity, hate speech, or explicit content in team names or branding.
- Teams may use abbreviations, but they must be clearly identifiable.
- Any name change requests must be submitted and approved by league admins.

### 3.3.4 Use of Ember League Branding

All social media graphics and other media using the Ember League branding must comply with the official style guide (to be provided by league admins). Teams are encouraged to maintain a consistent visual identity across platforms.

### 3.3.5 Enforcement & Penalties

- Failure to submit a proper logo results in a default placeholder being used.
- Use of restricted or inappropriate imagery may result in team disqualification.
- Repeated violations of branding policies may lead to penalties or removal from the league.

## 3.4 Official League Broadcast

- Selected matches will be cast and streamed on the Ember League's official Twitch/YouTube channel.
- Teams will be notified as soon as possible if their match is scheduled for broadcast.
- Players must be present in their designated Discord voice channel during broadcast games. Failure to comply results in a -2 ban penalty.
- Any delays or requests for rescheduling must be submitted to league admins at least 24 hours prior to the match.

### 3.4.1 Streamer Conduct

- Official casters and streamers are expected to maintain professionalism and neutrality.

# Section 4 - Season Format

## 4.1 Group Draw Show

The Group Draw Show will be held to determine the composition of groups for the league. It ensures a fair and transparent process for assigning teams to their respective groups.

### 4.1.1 Draw Show Procedure

**Seeding Process:**
- Teams will be seeded based on relevant factors such as previous performance, qualifiers, or average rank if applicable.
- Teams will be divided into Seed Pools (e.g., Pool 1, Pool 2) to ensure competitive balance.

**Draw Format:**
- Teams will be randomly drawn from each pool and assigned to groups in an alternating fashion via spinning wheel.
- No group shall have more than one team from the same organization.
- Adjustments may be made to avoid region or affiliation conflicts.

**Broadcast:** The draw will be publicly broadcasted on league channels for transparency. Casters or league officials will explain the draw process live.

**Tiebreaker Adjustment:** If an issue arises during the draw (e.g., conflicting teams), the league reserves the right to redraw from the affected pool.

**No Appeals:** Teams may not appeal the results of the draw. However, any administrative errors identified during the show will be addressed by league officials.

## 4.2 Regular Season Format

Matches occur on the listed days for each league at **8PM ET** unless both teams agree to a reschedule (see Section 7.1):

| Day | League |
|-----|--------|
| Monday | Cinder |
| Tuesday | Blaze & Scorch |
| Thursday | Magma |

The regular season length will be based on the amount of teams in each individual group and all regular season games will be **Best of 3 Fearless**.

### 4.2.1 Group Stage

- All teams will compete in scheduled matches within their assigned group.
- Each match is played as a Best of 3 series.
- Each team will play one Bo3 series per week over the course of the group stage, sometimes having a bye week or cross-division week that will not count towards the team's record.
- If there are 8 or more teams in any given League or Division, only the **top 6 teams** will advance to the playoffs.
- If a League or Division has 12 or more teams, the **top 8 teams** will advance to playoffs instead of 6.

## 4.3 Playoff Format

All playoff matches will be played as **Best of 5 Fearless**. Seeding for the playoffs bracket is determined by results of the Groups stage. Depending on the number of teams in the playoff bracket, 8 teams will make playoffs. If there are 8 or less teams in the league, some top seeds may receive byes.

## 4.4 Tiebreaker Criteria

When two teams are tied in Match Record, the following criteria is used to break the tie, **in order**:

1. **Head-to-Head Record** — The team who beat the other team in the regular season wins the tie.
2. **Game Win/Loss Differential** — The team with the higher individual game win percentage wins the tie.
3. **Total Game Wins** — The team with the highest number of individual game wins wins the tie.
4. **Fastest Average Win Time** — The team with the shortest average win duration wins the tie.
5. **Tiebreaker Match** — If necessary, a Bo1 tiebreaker match will be played.

## 4.5 Mid-Season Patch Policy

Newly released or reworked champions will be automatically banned for the next patch following their release before they are allowed to be played in the league. Additional information on any active champion bans will be posted in the #disabled-champs channel.

# Section 5 - Roster Management

## 5.1 Roster Limits and Changes

**Team member limitations:**
- Minimum: 5 players
- Maximum: 10 players (5 starters, 5 substitutes)

- Teams may add players before the roster lock deadline (24 hours before a match).
- Teams may make up to **three (3) roster changes per week**, which includes player additions and position swaps.
- The final roster lock for the regular season will be the day before the final game of the regular season.
- After this day, no roster changes may be made and any other roster change beyond this point is classified as use of an E-Sub (See Section 5.3).

**Solo Queue Mandate (Group Stage):**
Players must play a minimum of **5 Solo/Duo Queue Games every calendar week** to remain eligible to play. Any player in violation of this will count as an E-sub, and receive E-sub penalties.

**Solo Queue Mandate (Playoffs):**
Players must play a minimum of **3 Solo/Duo Queue Games every week** to remain eligible to play. Any player in violation of this will count as an E-sub, and receive E-sub penalties.

**Substitute Players:**
- Substitute Players are defined as players that are on a team's roster, but are not listed as a starter player.
- Teams may use a maximum of **three (3) substitutes** in a single match without penalty.
- Use of additional substitutes beyond this limit results in 2 bans lost for every game above 3 subs.
- Any substitute player added to a roster must be under the Original Cap of the League they are being added to.
- **Substitute players are NOT E-subs.** For info on E-subs, see Section 5.3.
- No new roster additions are allowed once playoffs begin.
- The final deadline for roster changes is **Saturday of the final regular-season week at 11:59 PM EST**.

## 5.2 Coaches

### 5.2.1 Coach Eligibility

- Each team may designate up to 2 coaches for their team.
- Coaches must be added prior to roster lock at the end of the regular season.
- Coaches do not count toward the player rank cap.

### 5.2.2 Coach Role and Access

- Coaches may join team communications before/between games to assist with drafts but once the game's loading screen commences they must leave the call.
- Coaches may not participate as a substitute player unless they are added to the roster as any other substitute would be. If they are not added 24 hours before the match occurs, they would be subject to E-Sub vetting and E-Sub penalties.

> **Note:** Substitutes on the team's roster may join the call for the same time period as coaches in between games but must leave at the same time.

### 5.2.3 Conduct and Responsibilities

- Coaches must uphold the same standards of behavior as players.
- Toxicity, harassment, or unsportsmanlike behavior from coaches will result in the same penalties for the team as if a player had committed the rulebreak.
- Coaches are encouraged to assist with team scheduling, communication, and improvement.
- Coaches may represent the team in official league communications if designated as a Team Captain or the Team Owner.

### 5.2.4 Coach Roster Changes

Coaches follow the same roster change rules and deadlines as players.

## 5.3 Emergency Substitute (E-Sub) Rule

**Definition:** An Emergency Substitute (E-Sub) is a player who is not part of the team's active roster or registered substitute list but is temporarily allowed to participate in a match due to unforeseen circumstances (e.g. last-minute absence, technical issues).

**Usage Limits:**
- An E-Sub may only be used with prior approval from league administrators.
- If three (3) or more E-Subs are attempted to be used in the same game, that team will forfeit the match automatically.
- Only 1 E-Sub will be allowed during playoffs per team per game.

**Eligibility:**
- The E-Sub must meet the League's Original Cap restrictions as listed in 2.1.1.
- E-Subs are not subject to the Absolute Cap afforded to regular rostered players and must currently qualify for their division.
- The E-Sub must not be on another roster within the same division.
- The E-Sub must not have been on any disqualified or eliminated roster during the current season.

**Procedure:**
- The team must notify league staff at least 1 hour before the match start time (exceptions may be made for emergencies).
- Staff must be notified via the Ticketing system or in the League's respective Roster Changes text channel.
- The E-Sub must be added to the team's Discord channel and summoner name submitted for verification.
- League staff will confirm or deny eligibility before match start.

**Ban Penalties:**
- 1 E-sub = 2 Bans Lost in First Ban Phase
- 2 E-subs = 4 Bans Lost (Three in First Ban Phase, One in Second Ban Phase)

**Penalties:** Unauthorized use of an E-Sub may result in match forfeiture, loss of deposit, or further disciplinary action.

## 5.4 Roster Recycling Restriction

To preserve the competitive integrity of the league:

- A player cannot be added to any other team within the league for a minimum of **7 days** after being dropped or removed from a team.
- This includes all teams and applies regardless of whether the player was officially active in matches.
- During the cooldown period, the player may be rostered on the new team but is not eligible to participate in official matches until the cooldown has expired.
- Any team found attempting to circumvent this rule may face penalties including reversal of roster changes, match forfeits, or disqualification.

### 5.4.1 Sister Teams

To maintain competitive integrity and prevent roster manipulation between affiliated (sister) teams, the Roster Recycling Restriction applies to all sister teams. Even if two teams are in the same Organization, the cooldown will still apply for that swap.

# Section 6 - Game Conduct

## 6.1 Gentlemen's Agreements

All matches must be played in accordance with official Ember League rules. Informal agreements ("gentlemen's agreements") will **NOT** be recognized by Ember Esports staff.

Any deviations from the rules without prior admin approval will result in both teams receiving a loss for that match during the regular season. If this is a Playoff game, the match will be replayed.

## 6.2 Voice Communication

- Players must use designated Ember League Discord voice channels.
- Only the five playing members may remain in the voice channel once the loading screen commences.

## 6.3 Competitive Integrity

- All players are expected to abide by Discord TOS and Riot Games Summoner's Code while competing.
- Cheating, scripting, or exploiting results in immediate disqualification.
- Sportsmanship violations (e.g., excessive toxicity) may result in ban losses or match forfeits.
- **No excessive BM:** All chatting must be limited to GG and minor communication with no ill intent.

## 6.4 Penalties for No-Show

Teams must be ready within 5 minutes of match time. A team is defined as ready when all 5 players are both in their designated Ember Esports Discord voice call **and** in the League client game lobby.

| Tardiness | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| 5–10 minutes late | Lose 1 Phase 1 ban |
| 11–15 minutes late | Lose 2 Phase 1 bans |
| 15–30 minutes late | Forfeit Game 1 |
| 31–45 minutes late | Forfeit Game 2 (auto-loss of Bo3) |
| 45+ minutes late | Forfeit Game 3 (auto-loss of Bo5) |

Teams that forfeit due to tardiness will not be permitted to reschedule unless explicitly approved by Admins. Excessive and/or repeated forfeits will result in removal from the League.

## 6.5 Side Selection, Drafting and Pre Game

Tournament codes must be used for every match. All teams must be present in their designated Ember League Discord Voice Channel and have their starting lineup ready at the scheduled match time. Players must be arranged in the League of Legends client from top to bottom in **LCS order: Top, Jungle, Mid, Bottom, Support**.

### 6.5.1 Side Selection

Side selection will be decided by a **1v1 or 2v2** between the two teams at the time designated for the series to begin, the winner earning **Side Selection Advantage (SSA)**. If both Team Captains agree, the 1v1 may be scheduled to a time before the series is set to begin. If either team does not wish to participate, an Ember League Staff Member will conduct a **coin flip** instead.

Side Selection Advantage is defined as the opportunity to select between playing on a side of your choice (Red or Blue) or getting the first pick in the draft. Loser gets SSA for all games after Game 1.

### 6.5.2 Side Selection 1v1/2v2

The side selection 1v1/2v2 will be the default way SSA is determined. For all streamed games, teams will **NOT** be able to opt out. All 1v1/2v2's will be played on **Howling Abyss** and will be **blind pick**. The three win conditions are:

- First blood (or first to 2 kills in a 2v2)
- First tower
- First to 100 farm

There will be a time limit of **10 minutes**; the player with more farm wins at that point. No mirror matches are allowed per Section 6.1. No Summoner Spell or item restrictions. The 1v1/2v2 may be streamed to your team, but all players/coaches must be muted to avoid coaching.

### 6.5.3 Drafting

Drafting should be handled with an external drafting site. Incorrect bans or picks could result in penalties, subject to Staff discretion. Once conducting in-client drafting, each player **MUST** pick the champion they are playing — no trades are permitted within the League client.

### 6.5.4 Role Swap Rule

Players must draft with the intent to play the roles declared on their team roster and may not swap roles without submitting an official roster change a minimum of **24 hours** prior to their game. If you are listed as a top laner, you must load in with the Top Lane Quest, etc.

Once loaded into the game, players may freely swap lanes and adjust gameplay based on matchups. The exception: swapping between ADC and Support is always allowed.

## 6.6 Pauses & Disconnects

**Acceptable Reasons to Pause:**
- Technical issues (hardware malfunction, game crash, internet disconnect)
- Verified client/server issues
- Player disconnect
- Emergency situations (within reason)

If a player is deemed unable to continue **before 10 seconds** into a game, the team may either continue 4v5 or use another rostered player in the same draft.

Each team is allowed **3 pause uses per game** and a total pause time of **10 minutes per game**.

**Combat Pause Rule:** Teams are not to pause during combat (any champion taking damage from another enemy champion in the last 3 seconds). Pausing in this window results in:

1. First offense: Warning
2. Second offense: Loss of pause privileges
3. Third offense: 2 Ban loss for 1 game

## 6.7 Disabled Champion Skins

Certain champion skins are not allowed due to visual clarity issues. Teams that notice a forbidden skin can request a remake within the first minute.

**Banned Skins:** Mecha Aatrox, Blackfrost Anivia, Project Ashe, Pulsefire Blitzcrank, True Damage Ekko, Arcade Ezreal, Pulsefire Ezreal, Vandal Gragas, Dragon Trainer Heimerdinger, FNATIC Jarvan IV, Forsaken Jayce, Dark Star Karma, Winter Wonder Karma, Elementalist Lux, Challenger Nidalee, Headhunter Nidalee, Nunu & Willump Bot, Zombie Nunu & Willump, Boneclaw Shyvana, Darkflame Shyvana, Arcade Sona, Atlantean Syndra, Arclight Varus, Dark Waters Vladimir, Night Bringer Vladimir.

# Section 7 - Scheduling

## 7.1 Streaming and Match Day

- Two teams will be designated to play on the official Match Day each week.
- These matches will be streamed on the league's official broadcast channels.
- Teams selected for broadcast will be notified at least **5 days** in advance.

**Flexible Scheduling:** All other group stage matches may be played at any time, as long as they are completed before Friday (subject to change). Match results must be reported immediately after completion.

**Failure to Schedule:** If teams fail to agree on a rescheduled match time, the match defaults back to **8 PM ET** on the original day. Failure to show up results in a game and/or series forfeit (See Section 6.4).

**Rescheduling:** Broadcast matches may only be rescheduled under exceptional circumstances with league approval. Non-broadcast matches can be rescheduled if both teams agree and the match is completed before the deadline. League admin must be notified.

## 7.2 Holidays

Matches will not be scheduled on the following recognized major holidays:

- New Year's Day
- Independence Day (U.S.)
- Thanksgiving Day (U.S.)
- Christmas Eve
- Christmas Day

Regional holidays may also be considered. Teams may request a reschedule if a match is set on an unrecognized holiday — requests must be submitted at least **7 days** in advance.

# Section 8 - Penalties and Appeals

## 8.1 Penalty Type Definitions

| Penalty Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Warning** | Issued for minor, non-malicious infractions |
| **Ban Loss** | Loss of champion ban(s) during the draft phase |
| **Player Suspension** | Player cannot compete in one or more matches |
| **Game Forfeit** | One game of a match is automatically lost |
| **Series Forfeit** | Entire match is forfeited |
| **Removal from League** | Player or team is disqualified from the season |

## 8.3 Reporting and Ticketing

If a rulebook violation is suspected, players/teams may report via the Ticketing system.

**For No-Shows and Tardies:** If on-call staff are available, the team captain should join the #Support-help-desk Voice Call. Otherwise, submit a ticket to the #tickets channel immediately with a screenshot showing both the League Client Lobby and the current time.

**For All Other Issues:** Tickets should be submitted promptly following a match and will be answered within 24-48 hours. Include as much detail as possible.

## 8.4 Appeals Process

Teams or players may appeal penalties by opening a ticket within **48 hours** of the ruling. Appeals should include:

- Description of the event or decision
- Screenshots, screen recordings, or other evidence
- Reasoning for reversal or reduction of the penalty

The Board will review appeals and respond within a reasonable timeframe. All decisions are final unless new evidence is provided.
